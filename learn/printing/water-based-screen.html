Water-Based Screen Print — Eco-friendly ink that integrates into the fabric

Ink typePigment / Water-based
Hand feelVery soft
Best forLight fabrics, large graphics
EcoLow VOC, no PVC

Water-based screen printing is the premium choice for t-shirt graphics on light-colored fabrics. Unlike plastisol, which sits on top of the fabric as a plastic layer, water-based ink penetrates into the fiber — becoming part of the fabric rather than a coating on it. The result is a print that feels almost like the fabric itself, with no rubber-like hand feel and no cracking over time.

This is the technique used by most premium basics brands for chest logos, large back graphics, and any print where hand feel is part of the product experience.

💡 Quick answer: Use water-based for light-colored fabrics where you want a soft, breathable print. Use plastisol for dark fabrics or when you need maximum color opacity and durability.

Water-based vs. plastisol

Water-Based
You are here — Soft feel
Ink integrates into fabric. Soft hand feel, breathable, eco-friendly. Best on white and light colors.
Plastisol
High opacity alternative
Sits on top of fabric. Vibrant on darks, durable, but thicker feel. Standard for bold graphics on dark tees.

Why it doesn't work well on dark fabrics

Water-based ink is translucent — it integrates with the fabric color beneath it rather than covering it. On a white or light-colored fabric, the ink color reads true. On a navy or black fabric, the ink is absorbed into the dark base and loses vibrancy — colors look washed out, and white becomes grey.

There are discharge variants of water-based ink that work on darks by removing the fabric dye before depositing the ink color, but this adds complexity and cost. For straightforward printing on dark fabrics, plastisol is the more practical choice.
